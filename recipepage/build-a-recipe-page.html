How to Make Beef Stew>

Join me, let's make beef

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs (700g) beef chuck, cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 3 potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • ½ tsp black pepper

Instructions

  • Prepare the Beef:
  • Sauté the Vegetables:
  • Deglaze & Simmer:
  • Add Vegetables & Thicken:
  • Finish & Serve:
  1. Prepare the Beef:

    Pat the beef chunks dry with a paper towel and season with salt and pepper. Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Sear the beef on all sides until browned, then remove and set aside.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables:

    In the same pot, add another tbsp of oil and sauté the onions until soft. Add garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant.

  3. Deglaze & Simmer:

    Stir in tomato paste, then pour in the red wine (if using) to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits. Add the beef back into the pot, followed by beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, rosemary, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Cover and let cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally.

  4. Add Vegetables & Thicken:

    Add carrots, potatoes, and celery. Let it simmer uncovered for another 30-40 minutes, or until vegetables are tender. If you want a thicker stew, mix 1 tbsp of flour with 2 tbsp of water and stir it into the pot.

  5. Finish & Serve:

    Remove the bay leaf and discard. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot!
